# Bay Area Hub Plan

Last updated: 2026-03-27

## Hub Page Strategy

Hub pages are regional entry points that route users into the strongest city pages and the quote flow. They reinforce local operational authority.

## Active Hub Pages

### 1. /areas — Service Area Center
- **Purpose**: Primary entry for "where do you serve?" queries
- **Content**: County-organized city cards with links
- **Priority**: Core Bay Area cities first, partner network compact
- **CTA**: Quote flow entry

### 2. /bay-area-dumpster-rental — Regional Authority Hub
- **Purpose**: Capture "bay area dumpster rental" search intent
- **Content**: Overview of Bay Area operations, links to all city pages
- **Priority**: 0.9 sitemap priority
- **Links to**: All Tier 1 + Tier 2 city pages

### 3. /dumpster-rental-east-bay — East Bay Regional Hub
- **Purpose**: Capture "east bay dumpster rental" intent
- **Content**: Oakland yard operations, East Bay city coverage
- **Yard**: Oakland
- **Cities**: Oakland, Berkeley, Alameda, San Leandro, Hayward, Walnut Creek, Concord, Richmond, Emeryville, Pleasanton, Dublin, Livermore

### 4. /dumpster-rental-south-bay — South Bay Regional Hub
- **Purpose**: Capture "south bay dumpster rental" intent
- **Content**: San Jose yard operations, Silicon Valley coverage
- **Yard**: San Jose
- **Cities**: San Jose, Santa Clara, Sunnyvale, Mountain View, Palo Alto, Milpitas, Cupertino, Fremont

### 5. /north-bay-dumpster-rental — North Bay Partner Hub

## Hub Content Rules

1. Route users to strongest city pages (Tier 1 first)
2. Include pricing CTAs (not just city lists)
3. Show operational credibility (yard info, fleet size, response times)
4. Avoid massive low-conversion city lists
5. Internal link to /quote, /pricing, /contractors
6. Reflect real Bay Area coverage only
